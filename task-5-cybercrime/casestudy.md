# Unauthorized Financial Activity Through Identity Theft: A Real Case Analysis

Identity theft is a type of cybercrime where someone’s personal information such as Aadhaar, PAN, or banking details is stolen and misused for financial gain. A real case reported in Mumbai shows how serious this problem can become.

In 2025, an engineer associated with IIT Bombay became a victim of identity theft after fraudsters accessed his personal documents, including his Aadhaar and PAN details. Using this information, they applied for three loans worth around ₹4 lakh and also issued four credit cards in his name without his knowledge. Over time, they carried out about 156 transactions and spent close to ₹10 lakh using those cards.

The victim only became aware of the fraud when banks and financial institutions started contacting him about unpaid EMIs. Until then, he had no idea that loans and credit cards had been issued in his name. When he checked his credit report, he found multiple unauthorized accounts and transactions linked to his identity. This confirmed that his personal information had been misused to impersonate him.

This case shows how identity theft usually happens in steps. First, attackers obtain personal data through sources such as phishing, data leaks, or unsecured sharing of documents. Then they use this information to open financial accounts or apply for loans. Working professionals and students are often targeted because they frequently share their documents online.

The consequences can be serious. Victims may suffer financial loss, damage to their credit score, and emotional stress. Resolving such cases can also take a long time. This incident highlights the importance of keeping personal information secure and regularly checking financial records.
